Stellaris Cortex-M3[edit]

Sandstorm (NRND)[edit]

Features[edit]

  • LM3S100 Series, LM3S300 Series, LM3S600 Series, LM3S800 Series
  • First ARM Cortex-M3 microcontrollers available anywhere
  • Feature sets start at $1
  • Up to 50MHz operation, Single-cycle memory up to 64K flash / 8K SRAM
  • Meticulous motion-control IP integration

Features[edit]

  • LM3S1000 Series, LM3S2000 Series, LM3S6000 Series, and LM3S8000 Series

  • Increasing single-cycle memory up to 256K flash / 64K SRAM
  • Further optimized for battery-backed applications
  • Added peripherals, such as additional UART, I2C, SSI, and QEI

DustDevil (NRND)[edit]

Features[edit]

  • Improving Stellaris offering with integration of USB OTG, Host, and Device options
  • Added DMA, improved GPIO drive strength, and additional PWM outputs
  • Additional fault protection inputs for advanced motion control
  • Bootloader and DriverLib in preloaded in ROM
  • Providing new small package options (64 pin LQFP)

Stellaris Cortex-M4[edit]

Blizzard[edit]

Features[edit]

  • All LM4F devices include the ARM Cortex-M4F core with the single precision floating-point capability at 80 MHz
  • Up to 256KB embedded flash memory and 32KB SRAM available
  • Up to 2 x 12-bit ADCs and 24 channels of inputs
  • Up to two CAN controllers
  • Optional full-speed USB 2.0 with device, host, and OTG
  • Advanced motion control capability, with up to 16 PWM outputs and two quadrature encoder interfaces
  • Generous serial communication options available:
  • Eight UARTs
  • Six I2C
  • Four SPI / SSI
  • Low-power modes. including power-saving hibernate
  • 64-LQFP, 100-LQFP, 144-LQFP, and 157-BGA packages

Device Series[edit]

  • LM4F110 Series: General MCU
  • LM4F120 Series: General MCU with USB device
  • LM4F130 Series: General MCU with USB OTG/host device
  • LM4F210 Series: General MCU with motion control
  • LM4F230 Series: General MCU with motion control and USB OTG/host device
