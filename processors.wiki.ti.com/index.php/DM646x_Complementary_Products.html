Introduction[edit]

This page serves as a repository of Complimentary Devices that can be paired with the DM646x SoC when integrating the device into a system. The Complementary Products listed here serve a wide range of functions such as:

  • Providing basic support functions for the SoC (power, clocking, etc.)
  • Expanding the interface options beyond the on-chip peripherals
  • Creating the link between the digital domains on the SoC to the system's analog/physical domains

The Complementary Products are organized into functional categories, with the goal of showing several options and their respective Benefits.


Analog Video Input[edit]

The DM646x includes a 8-/16-bit digital video input port (VPIF) that can be interfaced with a video decoder, or ADC, to bring analog SD and HD video into the SoC. Before bringing the analog video into the ADC, it is also necessary to run it through an anti-aliasing filter to restrict the bandwidth before sampling. Below is a sample of SD/HD video decoders and anti-aliasing filters that would be a good match for the DM646x. Analog Video Output[edit]

The DM646x includes a 8-/16-bit digital video output port (VPIF)that can be interfaced with a video encoder, or DAC, to deliver analog SD and HD video from the SoC. On the output of the ADC, it is also necessary to take the analog video through an amplifier and reconstruction filter to achieve the correct gain and remove the high-frequency images of the signal. Below is a sample of SD/HD video DACs, amplifiers, and reconstruction filters that would be a good match for the DM646x. Audio Input/Output[edit]

The DM646x includes a four-serializer Multi-channel Audio Serial Port (McASP) for interfacing to audio Codecs, ADCs, DACs, Digital Power Amplifiers, and more. In addition, the DM646x includes a second single-serializer McASP for outputting S/PDIF. The McASP1 output can directly source a coax S/PDIF output and can be connected to a fiber optic transmitting module to produce an optical S/PDIF output. RS-232[edit]

The DM646x UART peripherals can be used to implement a RS-232 interface when paired with the appropriate line driver/receiver. All three UARTs on the DM646x support transmit/receive and flow control features, but only UART0 is capable is supporting modem control capabilities. Below is a sampling of RS-232 products with varying numbers of drivers/receivers to support the desired RS-232 capabilities.

USB[edit]

The DM646x includes a USB 2.0 Port with an Integrated PHY. This port can function as a High-/Full-Speed Clientor a High-/Full-/Low-Speed Mini-Host (Supporting One External Device). An addition USB 2.0 port can be added to the system by interfacing an external USB Bridge Controller to the DM646x VLYNQ bus. Below is a sample USB Bridge Controller that would complement the DM646x.

Configurable for up to 4 Transmit Endpoints or up to 4 Receive Endpoints
Supports OTG Communications


PCI and PCI Express[edit]

The DM646x includes a 32-bit, 33MHz PCI peripheral that conforms to PCI Specification 2.3 and can operate as both a master and slave interface. Adding a PCI-to-PCI Bridge on this interface can allow transactions to occur between masters on one PCI bus and targets on another PCI bus. In addition, a bridge can be used to overcome the electrical loading limits of 10 devices per PCI bus and one PCI device per expansion slot by creating hierarchical buses.

The DM646x SoC can also be integrated into a PCI Express (PCIe) system when used in conjunction with a PCI-to-PCIe Bridge. Below is a sample PCI-to-PCI Bridge and PCI-to-PCIe Bridge that would complement the DM646x.

Clocking[edit]

The DM646x requires both a 27MHz main clock and a 24MHz USB clock. While crystals can be paired with the internal oscillators to provide these clocks, an external clock generator/synthesizer can sometime be more cost effective. Clock generators/synthesizers can use a single crystal to produce many clock output frequencies using their internal PLLs. Not only can they source both the 27MHz main clock and 24MHz USB clock for DM646x, they can also provide the clock for other devices in the system such as the audio and video encoders/decoders. Below is a sampling of clock generators/synthesizersthat would complement the DM646x. Also view the Clocks and Timing Selection Guide for a comprehensive guide to clocking solutions from TI.

Part Number Description Device Interface Benefits
CDCEL925 Programmable 2-PLL VCXO Clock Synthesizer Clock Input Provides DM646x Clocks and more

2 Internal PLLs and 5 1.8-V LVCMOS Outputs up to 160MHz
On-Chip VCXO with a Pull Range of ±150 ppm


Power[edit]

The DM646x requires regulated Core, 1.8V, and 3.3V voltages, sequenced in a low-to-high order. When selecting voltage regulators to pair with DM646x, TI provides a TMS320DM6467 Power Consumption Summary to help size the regulators for the worst case power of the system. In addition, TI provides a DM6467 Power Management Website with power reference designs using DCDC Controllers and Power Modules. Below is a sampling of the power management products that complement the DM646x. Also view the app note Enabling SmartReflex on the TMS320DM6467 for reference designs utilizing the power saving SmartReflex(TM) feature of DM646x.
